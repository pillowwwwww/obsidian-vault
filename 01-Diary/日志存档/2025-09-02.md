## Tracking

Let $f:\{0,1\}^n\to\{0,1\}$ be a Boolean function and let $\mu$ be any distribution on $\{0,1\}^n$. For any affine subspace $S\subseteq\{0,1\}^n$, define the (weighted) bias $$ \mathrm{bias}(f,\mu,S)\ \coloneqq\ \Big|\sum_{x\in S} (-1)^{f(x)}\,\mu(x)\Big|. $$ Define the (parity-type) discrepancy $$ \mathrm{disc}_{\mathsf{pt}}(f,\mu)\ \coloneqq\ -\log_2\!\Big(\max_{S}\mathrm{bias}(f,\mu,S)\Big). $$ Let $$ f^{\oplus k}(x_1,\ldots,x_k)\ \coloneqq\ f(x_1)\oplus\cdots\oplus f(x_k), \qquad \mu^{k}\ \text{be the \(k\)-fold product distribution of }\mu . $$ Problem. For all $f,\mu$ and integers $k\ge 1$, give the tightest two-sided inequality for $\mathrm{disc}_{\mathsf{pt}}\!\big(f^{\oplus k},\mu^{k}\big)$ in terms of $\mathrm{disc}_{\mathsf{pt}}(f,\mu)$, and specify the optimal constants explicitly (no $\Theta(\cdot)$ notation).



[Direct Sums for Parity Decision Trees](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.06552)


算法与复杂性

```
### 1

With $F_\mu,\widehat{F_\mu},\|\widehat{F_\mu}\|_\infty$ as above, for every affine subspace $S$,

$$

\boxed{\,\mathrm{bias}(f,\mu,S)\ \le\ \|\widehat{F_\mu}\|_\infty\, }.

$$

### 2

There exists an affine hyperplane $S^\star$ with

$$

\boxed{\,\mathrm{bias}(f,\mu,S^\star)\ \ge\ \tfrac12\,\|\widehat{F_\mu}\|_\infty\, }.

$$

### 3

Let $\chi_z(x)=(-1)^{\langle z,x\rangle}$ be Walsh characters on $\{0,1\}^n$. On the product space $(\{0,1\}^n)^k$, characters factorize coordinate-wise:

$$

\boxed{\ \chi_{(z_1,\dots,z_k)}(x_1,\dots,x_k)\ =\ \prod_{i=1}^k \chi_{z_i}(x_i)\ }.

$$
```



```
step 1 — Set up notation (e-log scale and a spectral shorthand)

Define

disce(f,μ) := −ln⁡ ⁣(max⁡Sbias(f,μ,S)),A := −ln⁡∥Fμ^∥∞.\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)\ :=\ -\ln\!\Big(\max_{S}\mathrm{bias}(f,\mu,S)\Big),\qquad A\ :=\ -\ln\big\|\widehat{F_\mu}\big\|_\infty.

step 2 — Lower endpoint for the single-instance (Concept 1)

By Concept 1, for every affine SS,

bias(f,μ,S)≤∥Fμ^∥∞\mathrm{bias}(f,\mu,S)\le \|\widehat{F_\mu}\|_\infty.

Taking the maximum over SS and applying −ln⁡(⋅)-\ln(\cdot) (decreasing on (0,1](0,1]),

disce(f,μ) ≥ A.\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)\ \ge\ A.

step 3 — Upper endpoint for the single-instance (Concept 2)

By Concept 2, there exists a hyperplane S⋆S^\star with

bias(f,μ,S⋆)≥12 ∥Fμ^∥∞\mathrm{bias}(f,\mu,S^\star)\ge \tfrac12\,\|\widehat{F_\mu}\|_\infty.

Again applying −ln⁡-\ln,

disce(f,μ) ≤ −ln⁡∥Fμ^∥∞+ln⁡2 = A+ln⁡2.\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)\ \le\ -\ln\|\widehat{F_\mu}\|_\infty+\ln 2\ =\ A+\ln 2.

step 4 — Collect the single-instance interval

From steps 2–3:

disce(f,μ) ∈ [A, A+ln⁡2] ⟺A ∈ [disce(f,μ)−ln⁡2, disce(f,μ)].(★)\boxed{\ \mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)\ \in\ [A,\ A+\ln 2]\ }\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad A\ \in\ \big[\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)-\ln 2,\ \mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)\big]. \tag{★}

step 5 — Explicit product form of FμkF_{\mu^k} (clarification ①)

step 6 — Fourier coefficient factorization for the kk-fold object 

Let χz(x)=(−1)⟨z,x⟩\chi_z(x)=(-1)^{\langle z,x\rangle} be Walsh characters.

By Concept3 (character factorization), on ({0,1}n)k(\{0,1\}^n)^k,

χ(z1,…,zk)(x1,…,xk)=∏i=1kχzi(xi).\chi_{(z_1,\dots,z_k)}(x_1,\dots,x_k)=\prod_{i=1}^k \chi_{z_i}(x_i).

Therefore, using the definition

Fμk^(z1,…,zk)=2−nk∑x1,…,xkFμk(x1,…,xk) χ(z1,…,zk)(x1,…,xk)\widehat{F_{\mu^k}}(z_1,\dots,z_k)=2^{-nk}\sum_{x_1,\dots,x_k} F_{\mu^k}(x_1,\dots,x_k)\,\chi_{(z_1,\dots,z_k)}(x_1,\dots,x_k),

and the product form from step 5, we get a product of kk separate sums:

Fμk^(z1,…,zk)=∏i=1kFμ^(zi).\widehat{F_{\mu^k}}(z_1,\dots,z_k)=\prod_{i=1}^k \widehat{F_\mu}(z_i).

step 7 — From coefficient factorization to an L∞L_\infty power (clarification ②)

Because the index sets are finite, maxima are attained. Hence, with

a(z):=∣Fμ^(z)∣≥0a(z):=|\widehat{F_\mu}(z)|\ge0,

∥Fμk^∥∞=max⁡(z1,…,zk) ∏i=1ka(zi)=(max⁡za(z))k=∥Fμ^∥∞ k.\big\|\widehat{F_{\mu^k}}\big\|_\infty =\max_{(z_1,\dots,z_k)}\ \prod_{i=1}^k a(z_i) =\Big(\max_{z} a(z)\Big)^{k} =\big\|\widehat{F_\mu}\big\|_\infty^{\,k}.

Define

B := −ln⁡∥Fμk^∥∞,B\ :=\ -\ln\big\|\widehat{F_{\mu^k}}\big\|_\infty,

then

B = kA .(†)\boxed{\ B\ =\ kA\ }. \tag{†}

step 8 — Apply Concepts 1 & 2 to the kk-fold instance

Repeating the single-instance reasoning (steps 2–3) for (f⊕k,μk)(f^{\oplus k},\mu^k),

disce(f⊕k,μk) ∈ [B, B+ln⁡2].\mathrm{disc}_e(f^{\oplus k},\mu^k)\ \in\ [B,\ B+\ln 2].

Using (†) this is

disce(f⊕k,μk) ∈ [kA, kA+ln⁡2].\mathrm{disc}_e(f^{\oplus k},\mu^k)\ \in\ [kA,\ kA+\ln 2].

step 9 — Substitute the range of AA and scale the interval

From (★), A∈[disce(f,μ)−ln⁡2, disce(f,μ)]A\in[\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)-\ln2,\ \mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)].

Multiplying by k≥1k\ge1 preserves order:

kA ∈ [k(disce(f,μ)−ln⁡2), k disce(f,μ)].kA\ \in\ \big[k(\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)-\ln 2),\ k\,\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)\big].

Thus

disce(f⊕k,μk) ∈ [k(disce(f,μ)−ln⁡2), k disce(f,μ)+ln⁡2] .\boxed{\ \mathrm{disc}_e(f^{\oplus k},\mu^k)\ \in\ \big[k(\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)-\ln 2),\ k\,\mathrm{disc}_e(f,\mu)+\ln 2\big]\ }.

step 10 — Convert back to base-2 discrepancy (final answer)

By definition discpt=disce/ln⁡2\mathrm{disc}_{\mathsf{pt}}=\mathrm{disc}_e/\ln 2, divide the endpoints by ln⁡2\ln 2:

k discpt(f,μ)−k ≤ discpt(f⊕k,μk) ≤ k discpt(f,μ)+1 .\boxed{\ k\,\mathrm{disc}_{\mathsf{pt}}(f,\mu)-k\ \le\ \mathrm{disc}_{\mathsf{pt}}(f^{\oplus k},\mu^k)\ \le\ k\,\mathrm{disc}_{\mathsf{pt}}(f,\mu)+1\ }.
```
