## Tracking

```
Step 1 (Concept 1).

For each block, when $r=\lceil 2^m\ln 2\rceil$,

$$

p:=\Pr[\mathrm{Tribes}(A_i)=1]=\tfrac12+o(1).

$$

Write $Z_i:=\mathrm{Tribes}(A_i)\in\{0,1\}$.

Step 2 (Instruction premise (2) + Concept 3).

Let $S_m:=\sum_{i=1}^m Z_i$. Under the uniform source, $\Pr[S_m=m/2]=\Theta(m^{-1/2})$; the majority with threshold $m/2+1$ flips only on the “middle layer” $|a|=m/2$ (pivot identity). Since source $D$ differs from $U$ only on this event, the single-block expectation gap satisfies

$$

\delta:=\big|\mathbb E_D[H]-\mathbb E_U[H]\big|=\Theta(m^{-1/2}).

$$

Step 3 (Concept 4).

Hoeffding amplification then yields the repetitions needed for constant advantage:

$$

t=\Theta(1/\delta^2)=\Theta(m).

$$

Step 4 (Concepts 2 + 5).

$\mathrm{Tribes}$ has a minimal 1-certificate “one all-1 column” of size $m$; hence

$$

\mathrm{width}(\mathrm{Tribes})=C_1(\mathrm{Tribes})=m.

$$

Step 5 (Concepts 7 + 6).

For $h(z)=\mathbf 1[\sum z_i\ge m/2+1]$, $C_1(h)=\tfrac m2+1=\Theta(m)$. By block-product composition,

$$

C_1(H)\ \le\ C_1(h)\cdot C_1(\mathrm{Tribes})=\Theta(m)\cdot m=\Theta(m^2),

$$

so $\mathrm{width}(H)\le m^2$.

Step 6 (Instruction premise (1): L12).

With $F_t=g\circ H^{\,t}$ and $g$ a monotone threshold,

$$

\mathrm{width}(F_t)\ \le\ t\cdot \mathrm{width}(H)\ \le\ t\,m^2.

$$

Step 7 (Size conversion; using $r=\Theta(2^m)$ from Concept 1).

The total input length is $n=t\,m^2 r=\Theta(m)\cdot m^2\cdot \Theta(2^m)=\Theta(m^3 2^m)$. Solving gives $m=\Theta(\log n)$. Therefore

$$

\mathrm{width}(F_t)\ \le\ t\,m^2=\Theta(m^3)=\Theta\big((\log n)^3\big).

$$

Step 8 (Minimal $t$).

From Step 3 and $m=\Theta(\log n)$, the minimal order is $t=\Theta(m)=\Theta(\log n)$.

Final.

Minimal order of $t$: $\Theta(\log n)$.

Upper bound on the DNF width of $F_t$: $\Theta((\log n)^3)$.
```
