Concept/Theorem:

---

### concept1) QSMP message template and accounting

Each party can locally prepare a **code-space phase state** or a **zero-set superposition** over $\Sigma^n\simeq(\mathbb F_q)^N$:

$$
\lvert \psi_u\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{|C_n|}}\sum_{c\in C_n}\omega^{\langle c,u\rangle}\lvert c\rangle,\quad
\lvert \phi_S\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{|S|}}\sum_{e\in S}\lvert e\rangle.
$$

Encoding a register over $(\mathbb F_q)^N$ costs $Q=N\log_2 q$ qubits per message; with folded-RS parameters $N,\log q=\mathrm{poly}(n)$, the total QSMP cost (Alice + Bob, one shot each) is **$\mathrm{poly}(n)$**.

---

### concept2) Deterministic list decoding for GRS (Guruswami–Sudan)

There is a polynomial-time procedure $\textsf{ListDecode}(z)$ that outputs

$$
\{x\in \mathrm{GRS}_{q,\gamma,k,\mathbf v}:\ \mathrm{hw}(x-z)\le N-\sqrt{kN}\},
$$

with $N=q-1,\ d=N-k+1$. Treat it as a callable black-box.

---

### concept3) List-recoverability of folded RS

For $C=\mathrm{RS}^{(m)}_{\mathbb F_q,\gamma,k}\subseteq\Sigma^n$ (with $\Sigma=\mathbb F_q^m,\ n=N/m$): if integers $r,s\ge1$ satisfy

$$
\frac{\zeta N}{m}\ \ge\ \Bigl(1+\frac{s}{r}\Bigr)\Bigl(N\ell k^s\Bigr)^{\!\frac{1}{s+1}}\frac{1}{m-s+1},\qquad
(r+s)\Bigl(\frac{N\ell}{k}\Bigr)^{\!\frac{1}{s+1}}<q,
$$

then $C$ is $(\zeta,\ell,L)$-list-recoverable with $L\le q^s$. (Interface: for given $(N,q,m,k,\zeta,\ell)$, choose $(r,s)$ to read off $L$.)

---

### concept4) High-probability unique decodability of the dual under biased noise

Let $e\in(\mathbb F_q)^N$ have i.i.d. coordinates with $\Pr[e_j=0]=1-p$, $\Pr[e_j=a\neq0]=\tfrac{p}{q-1}$. For $z=x+e$ with unknown $x\in C_n^\perp$, define $\textsf{Decode}(z)$: unfold, run $\textsf{ListDecode}$, and return the unique $x$ if $\mathrm{hw}(z-x)\le(p+\varepsilon)N$. Whenever

$$
N-\sqrt{kN}\ \ge\ (p+\varepsilon)N,
$$

we have $\Pr[\textsf{Decode}(z)=x]\ge 1-2^{-\Omega(N)}$.

---

### concept5) Quantitative subcube normalization (communication $\Rightarrow$ fixed coordinates + min-entropy)

Any public-coin two-way protocol with total communication $T$ bits can be normalized so that each accepting rectangle $X\times Y\subseteq\Sigma^n\times\Sigma^n$ fixes two coordinate sets $I,J$ with

$$
|I|+|J|\ \le\ \frac{c\,T}{\log_2|\Sigma|},
$$

and every unfixed coordinate has min-entropy at least $\alpha=\Omega(1)$ (i.e., $\max_a\Pr[v=a]\le 2^{-\alpha}$). Treat $c>0$ as an absolute constant tied to the normalization.

---

### concept6) Entropy–list tradeoff: hitting bound for dangerous patterns

Suppose on an accepting rectangle the set of **dangerous candidates** has size $\le L$, and the remaining $t'$ unfixed coordinates each have min-entropy $\ge \alpha$. Then for any fixed dangerous pattern,

$$
\Pr[\text{unfixed coordinates hit that pattern}]\ \le\ 2^{-\alpha t'}.
$$

By a union bound over all dangerous candidates,

$$
\Pr[\text{hit some dangerous pattern}]\ \le\ L\cdot 2^{-\alpha t'}.
$$

(Interface: you will compare $\log_2 L$ against $\alpha t'$.)

---

### concept7) Direct-product amplifier for $t$ independent copies

If a single copy (under the normalized distribution) has success probability $\le \rho(T)$ at communication $T$, then for $t$ independent copies any protocol of total communication $T_{\rm tot}$ with $T_{\rm tot}\log T_{\rm tot}\le c'\,t\,T$ satisfies

$$
\Pr[\text{solve all }t\text{ copies}]\ \le\ \rho(T)^{\,\Theta(t)}\ =\ 2^{-\Omega(t)}.
$$

---

### concept8) $k$-wise independent hash families (explicit, size vs. seed)

Over $\mathbb F_r$, the degree-$(k\!-\!1)$ polynomial family $h(i)=P(i)$ is $k$-wise independent, with seed length $\Theta(k\log r)$ and family size $|\mathcal H|=r^k$. When the **same** hash $h$ is used across $t$ copies, a union bound over $|\mathcal H|$ keeps failure negligible provided

$$
\beta t\ -\ \log_2|\mathcal H|\ \gg\ 1,
$$

where $2^{-\beta t}$ upper-bounds the per-$h$ failure from the direct-product step.
