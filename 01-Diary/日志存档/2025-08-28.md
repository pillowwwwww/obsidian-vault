## Concepts

**Concept 1 — Tribes probability and balancing**  
$\Pr[\mathrm{Tribes}(A)=1]=1-(1-2^{-m})^{r}$. With $r=\lceil 2^{m}\ln 2\rceil$, this equals $1/2+o(1)$.

**Concept 2 — 1-certificate of Tribes**  
Pick one column and fix all its $m$ bits to 1 $\Rightarrow$ minimal 1-certificate size of $\mathrm{Tribes}$ is $m$.

**Concept 3 — Central binomial layer (lite)**  
If $S_m\sim\mathrm{Bin}(m,1/2)$, then $\Pr[S_m=m/2]=\Theta(m^{-1/2})$.

**Concept 4 — Pivot identity for majority**  
For $\operatorname{MAJ}_m$, flipping one coordinate changes the output iff the other $m{-}1$ sum equals $m/2$. The flip probability equals $\Pr[S_m=m/2]$.

**Concept 5 — Hoeffding amplification**  
Majority vote reaches constant distinguishing advantage with $t=\Theta(1/\delta^2)$ for expectation gap $\delta>0$.

**Concept 6 — DNF width equals maximal 1-certificate size**  
Minimal DNF width of $f$ equals $C_1(f)$.

**Concept 7 — Certificate composition under block product**  
For $F=g\circ(f,\ldots,f)$ on disjoint variable blocks: $C_1(F)\le C_1(g),C_1(f)$.

**Concept 8 — Certificate size of a threshold**  
For $g(z)=\mathbf 1[\sum z_i\ge k]$, $C_1(g)=k$.

**Concept 9 — Exponential–polynomial size accounting (lite)**  
If $r=\Theta(2^m)$ and $n=t\cdot m^2 r$ with $t=\Theta(m)$, then

n=Θ(m32m),m≤log⁡2n,m≥log⁡2n−3log⁡2m−O(1).n=\Theta(m^3 2^m),\qquad m\le \log_2 n,\qquad m\ge \log_2 n - 3\log_2 m - O(1).
