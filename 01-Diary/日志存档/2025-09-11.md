## # Concepts — Pack S（细节最全；最容易得出正确 0.0525）

**C1 | Quadratic “sandwich” and substitution for the averaged-system term**  
From c1∥x∥2≤V≤c2∥x∥2c_1\|x\|^2\le V\le c_2\|x\|^2 and ∂xV fav≤−c3∥x∥2\partial_x V\,f_{\mathrm{av}}\le -c_3\|x\|^2, use

− c3∥x∥2 ≤ −c3c2 V,∥x∥2 ≤ V/c1.-\,c_3\|x\|^2\ \le\ -\tfrac{c_3}{c_2}\,V,\qquad \|x\|^2\ \le\ V/c_1.

**C2 | Vanishing-perturbation gives the ε\varepsilon-linear terms in V˙\dot V**

V˙ ≤ −αV + ε σ(∥y∥),\dot V\ \le\ -\alpha V\ +\ \varepsilon\,\sigma(\|y\|),

with ∥g∥≤δ1∥y∥+δ2∥y∥ ∥u˙s∥\|g\|\le \delta_1\|y\|+\delta_2\|y\|\,\|\dot u_s\| and ∥∂xV∥≤c5∥y∥\|\partial_xV\|\le c_5\|y\|, yielding

ε(c5δ1c1)Vandε(c5δ2c1)V ∥u˙s∥.\varepsilon\Big(\tfrac{c_5\delta_1}{c_1}\Big)V \quad\text{and}\quad \varepsilon\Big(\tfrac{c_5\delta_2}{c_1}\Big)V\,\|\dot u_s\|.

**C3 | Total variation (TV) as a unified exponential measure — use the supremum weight**  
Define

∫t1t2 ⁣∥dus∥=∑ ⁣∫∥u˙s∥ dt+∑∥Δus∥,∫∥dus∥≤μ(t2−t1)+α.\int_{t_1}^{t_2}\!\|du_s\|=\sum\!\int\|\dot u_s\|\,dt+\sum\|\Delta u_s\|,\qquad \int\|du_s\|\le \mu(t_2-t_1)+\alpha .

To merge **flow** and **jump** into one factor ek∫∥dus∥e^{k\int\|du_s\|}, use the **supremum (max) coefficient** across segments:

TV weight=max⁡{c5δ2c1, c5ϕc1} for the ε-linear part,c4c1 for the constant part.\text{TV weight}=\max\Big\{\tfrac{c_5\delta_2}{c_1},\ \tfrac{c_5\phi}{c_1}\Big\}\ \text{for the \(\varepsilon\)-linear part},\qquad \tfrac{c_4}{c_1}\ \text{for the constant part}.

**C4 | First-order relative jump increment (state and parameter contributions)**  
If ∥y+−y−∥≤εϕ ∥Δus∥ ∥y−∥\|y^+-y^-\|\le \varepsilon\phi\,\|\Delta u_s\|\,\|y^-\|, ∥∂xV∥≤c5∥y∥\|\partial_xV\|\le c_5\|y\|, ∥∂usV∥≤c4∥y∥2\|\partial_{u_s}V\|\le c_4\|y\|^2, and V≥c1∥y∥2V\ge c_1\|y\|^2, then

V+−V−V− ≤ (c5ϕc1 ε+c4c1) ∥Δus∥.\frac{V^+-V^-}{V^-}\ \le\ \Big(\tfrac{c_5\phi}{c_1}\,\varepsilon+\tfrac{c_4}{c_1}\Big)\,\|\Delta u_s\|.

**C5 | Lipschitz-gradient (L-smooth) second-order remainder — choose κ=LV\kappa=L_V**  
If ∇V\nabla V is LVL_V-Lipschitz, then

V(y+Δy) ≤ V(y)+∇V(y)⊤Δy+κ ∥Δy∥2,κ≥LV2.V(y+\Delta y)\ \le\ V(y)+\nabla V(y)^\top\Delta y+\kappa\,\|\Delta y\|^2,\quad \kappa\ge \tfrac{L_V}{2}.

Use κ=LV\kappa=L_V. With ∥y+−y−∥≤εϕ∥Δus∥ ∥y−∥\|y^+-y^-\|\le \varepsilon\phi\|\Delta u_s\|\,\|y^-\|, ∥Δus∥≤diam(Γ)\|\Delta u_s\|\le \mathrm{diam}(\Gamma), and V≥c1∥y∥2V\ge c_1\|y\|^2, the **quadratic** coefficient is

ℓ2=LV ϕ2 diam(Γ)c1,hence the contributionμ ℓ2 ε2.\ell_2=\tfrac{L_V\,\phi^2\,\mathrm{diam}(\Gamma)}{c_1}, \quad\text{hence the contribution}\quad \mu\,\ell_2\,\varepsilon^2.

## # Concepts — Pack M（省略关键选择；正确率中等）

**C1 | Quadratic bounds and averaged-term inequality**  
Assume c1∥x∥2≤V≤c2∥x∥2c_1\|x\|^2\le V\le c_2\|x\|^2 and ∂xV fav≤−c3∥x∥2\partial_x V\,f_{\mathrm{av}}\le -c_3\|x\|^2.  
Both substitutions −c3∥x∥2→ -c_3\|x\|^2\to a bound in terms of VV and ∥x∥2≤V/c1\|x\|^2\le V/c_1 are available.

**C2 | Vanishing-perturbation linear terms in V˙\dot V**

V˙ ≤ −αV + ε σ(∥y∥),σ(∥y∥) from bounds on ∥∂xV∥ and ∥g∥.\dot V\ \le\ -\alpha V\ +\ \varepsilon\,\sigma(\|y\|),\quad \sigma(\|y\|)\ \text{from bounds on }\|\partial_xV\|\ \text{and}\ \|g\|.

With ∥g∥≤δ1∥y∥+δ2∥y∥ ∥u˙s∥\|g\|\le \delta_1\|y\|+\delta_2\|y\|\,\|\dot u_s\| and ∥∂xV∥≤c5∥y∥\|\partial_xV\|\le c_5\|y\|:

ε(c5δ1c1)Vandε(c5δ2c1)V ∥u˙s∥.\varepsilon\Big(\tfrac{c_5\delta_1}{c_1}\Big)V \quad\text{and}\quad \varepsilon\Big(\tfrac{c_5\delta_2}{c_1}\Big)V\,\|\dot u_s\|.

**C3 | Total variation (TV) as unified exponential measure**

∫t1t2 ⁣∥dus∥=∑ ⁣∫∥u˙s∥ dt+∑∥Δus∥,∫∥dus∥≤μ(t2−t1)+α.\int_{t_1}^{t_2}\!\|du_s\|=\sum\!\int\|\dot u_s\|\,dt+\sum\|\Delta u_s\|,\qquad \int\|du_s\|\le \mu(t_2-t_1)+\alpha .

Use a single exponential factor ek∫∥dus∥e^{k\int\|du_s\|} with a coefficient kk that **dominates** both flow and jump contributions over the interval.

**C4 | First-order relative jump increment**  
If the stated jump, gradient, and Lyapunov bounds hold, then

V+−V−V− ≤ (c5ϕc1 ε+c4c1) ∥Δus∥.\frac{V^+-V^-}{V^-}\ \le\ \Big(\tfrac{c_5\phi}{c_1}\,\varepsilon+\tfrac{c_4}{c_1}\Big)\,\|\Delta u_s\|.

**C5 | L-smooth second-order remainder (any admissible κ≥LV/2\kappa\ge L_V/2)**

V(y+Δy) ≤ V(y)+∇V(y)⊤Δy+κ ∥Δy∥2,κ≥LV2.V(y+\Delta y)\ \le\ V(y)+\nabla V(y)^\top\Delta y+\kappa\,\|\Delta y\|^2,\quad \kappa\ge \tfrac{L_V}{2}.

This yields a **quadratic** contribution in ε\varepsilon with coefficient proportional to κ ϕ2 diam(Γ)/c1\kappa\,\phi^2\,\mathrm{diam}(\Gamma)/c_1.

## # Concepts — Pack H（省略更多细节；高方差）

**C1 | Quadratic bounds and averaged-system inequality available**  
Assume c1∥x∥2≤V≤c2∥x∥2c_1\|x\|^2\le V\le c_2\|x\|^2 and ∂xV fav≤−c3∥x∥2\partial_x V\,f_{\mathrm{av}}\le -c_3\|x\|^2.  
Use these relations as needed when converting between ∥x∥\|x\| and VV.

**C2 | Vanishing-perturbation structure for V˙\dot V**

V˙ ≤ −αV + ε σ(∥y∥),\dot V\ \le\ -\alpha V\ +\ \varepsilon\,\sigma(\|y\|),

with σ(∥y∥)\sigma(\|y\|) formed from bounds on ∥∂xV∥\|\partial_xV\| and ∥g∥\|g\|.

**C3 | TV measure for càdlàg inputs**

∫t1t2 ⁣∥dus∥=∑ ⁣∫∥u˙s∥ dt+∑∥Δus∥,∫∥dus∥≤μ(t2−t1)+α.\int_{t_1}^{t_2}\!\|du_s\|=\sum\!\int\|\dot u_s\|\,dt+\sum\|\Delta u_s\|,\qquad \int\|du_s\|\le \mu(t_2-t_1)+\alpha .

Aggregate the flow and jump effects over the interval into a single exponential factor.

**C4 | Jump-induced first-order change in VV**  
Under the stated jump and Lyapunov bounds,

V+−V−V− ≲ (c5ϕc1 ε+c4c1) ∥Δus∥.\frac{V^+-V^-}{V^-}\ \lesssim\ \Big(\tfrac{c_5\phi}{c_1}\,\varepsilon+\tfrac{c_4}{c_1}\Big)\,\|\Delta u_s\|.

**C5 | Second-order remainder under Lipschitz gradient**  
If ∇V\nabla V is LVL_V-Lipschitz, there exists κ≥LV2\kappa\ge \tfrac{L_V}{2} such that

V(y+Δy) ≤ V(y)+∇V(y)⊤Δy+κ ∥Δy∥2.V(y+\Delta y)\ \le\ V(y)+\nabla V(y)^\top\Delta y+\kappa\,\|\Delta y\|^2.

With bounded ∥Δus∥\|\Delta u_s\|, this produces a quadratic contribution in ε\varepsilon to the overall exponential rate.
