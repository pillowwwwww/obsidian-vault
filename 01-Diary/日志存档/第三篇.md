Step 1 (Concept 1).  
For each $A_i$, when $r=\lceil 2^m\ln2\rceil$ we have  
$\Pr[\mathrm{Tribes}(A_i)=1]=\tfrac12+o(1)$.  
Let $Z_i:=\mathrm{Tribes}(A_i)$.

Step 2 (Concept 3 + 4).  
Let $S_m=\sum_{i=1}^m Z_i$. Under the uniform distribution, $\Pr[S_m=m/2]=\Theta(m^{-1/2})$.  
The majority with threshold $\tfrac m2+1$ is flipped only at the “pivotal layer” $|a|=m/2$. Therefore for a single block

$$
\delta \;:=\; \big|\mathbb E_D[H]-\mathbb E_U[H]\big|\;=\;\Theta(m^{-1/2}).
$$

Step 3 (Concept 5).  
To amplify the advantage to $\Omega(1)$:  
$t=\Theta(1/\delta^2)=\Theta(m)$. (minimal order)

Step 4 (Concept 2 + 6).  
The minimal 1-certificate size of $\mathrm{Tribes}$ is $m$, hence $\mathrm{width}(\mathrm{Tribes})=m$.

Step 5 (Concept 8 + 7 + 6).  
For the threshold $h(z)=\mathbf 1[\sum z_i\ge m/2+1]$, we have $C_1(h)=\tfrac m2+1=\Theta(m)$.  
By block composition we obtain  
$C_1(H)\le C_1(h)\cdot C_1(\mathrm{Tribes})=\Theta(m)\cdot m=\Theta(m^2)$,  
thus $\mathrm{width}(H)\le m^2$.

Step 6 (L12).  
$F_t=g\circ H^{\,t}$ with $g$ a monotone threshold function,  
$\mathrm{width}(F_t)\le t\cdot \mathrm{width}(H)\le t\,m^2$.

Step 7 (Scaling; using Concept 1 with $r=\Theta(2^m)$).  
The total input length is $n=t\,m^2 r=\Theta(m)\cdot m^2\cdot \Theta(2^m)=\Theta(m^3 2^m)$.  
Hence $m=\Theta(\log n)$, and therefore

$$
\mathrm{width}(F_t)\ \le\ t\,m^2\ =\ \Theta(m^3)\ =\ O\big((\log n)^3\big).
$$

Final.  
Minimal order of $t$: $\Theta(\log n)$.  
Upper bound on DNF width of $F_t$: $O((\log n)^3)$.
